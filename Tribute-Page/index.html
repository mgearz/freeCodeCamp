Tim Burton

Director • Artist • Visionary

Timeline Highlights

  • 1958 – Burton is born in Burbank, California
  • 1971 – A 13-year-old Tim creates his first known film, "The Island of Doctor Agor"
  • 1975 – Began school in the Character Animation Program at CalArts
  • 1980 – Became an apprentice at Walt Disney Animation
  • 1982 – Created his first animated short film, "Vincent"
  • 1985 – Feature-length directorial debut, "Pee Wee's Big Adventure"
  • 1988 – Directs fantasy-horror-comedy, "Beetlejuice"
  • 1989 – Kickstarted the superhero film genre with "Batman" starring Michael Keaton
  • 1990 – Writes and Directs "Edward Scissorhands"
  • 1992 – "Batman Returns" in Burton's blockbuster sequel
  • 1993 – Writes and Produces the stop-motion animated classic, "Nightmare Before Christmas"
  • 1994 – Directs a bio-pic for cult filmmaker, "Ed Wood"
  • 1996 – Tackles sci-fi by directing "Mars Attacks"
  • 1999 – Brings the tale of "Sleepy Hollow" to the big screen
  • 2001 – Reimagines "The Planet of the Apes"
  • 2005 – Directs the second adaptation of "Charlie and the Chocolate Factory"
  • 2005 – Co-Directs the stop-motion animated musical, "Corpse Bride"
  • 2007 – Brought the Broadway Musical "Sweeney Todd" to the silver screen
  • 2009 – A retrospective gallery of his work goes on display at MoMa, "The World of Tim Burton"
  • 2010 – Creates a live-action adaptation of "Alice in Wonderland"
  • 2012 – Releases "Dark Shadows" and a feature-length version of "Frankenweenie"
  • 2014 – Directs biopic about American artist Margaret Keane, "Big Eyes"
  • 2016 – Directs "Miss Peregrin's School for Peculiar Childern"
  • 2019 – Directs live-action adaptation of "Dumbo"
  • 2019 – His exhibition "Lost Vegas" opens at The Neon Museum in Las Vegas
  • 2022 – Directed and produced a Netflix show, "Wednesday", based on the Addam's Family

"Visions are worth fighting for. Why spend your life making someone else's dreams?"
– Tim Burton
